A comprehensive portfolio detailing the strategic frameworks, health financing, and current SRMNEA-Nut indicators in Cameroon to ensure healthy human capital.

Situation of Health Financing

Health expenditure was evaluated at approximately 34,589 FCFA per capita (2018). The financing system faces challenges with high out-of-pocket costs.

  • ~70% Households (Out-of-pocket)
  • ~15% State / Public Admin
  • ~15% External Partners (PTF)

The GFF Investment Case identified a financing gap of 57.3% for the 2018-2022 period.

RMNCAH Strategic Framework

The PSN-SRMNIA-Nut 2024-2030 aims to minimize mortality and morbidity through three main strategic axes:

Axis 1: High-Impact Interventions

Extending the coverage of high-impact interventions to reduce morbidity and mortality for mothers, newborns, children, and adolescents.

Axis 2: Quality & Accessibility

Strengthening support activities to ensure the permanent availability, accessibility, and utilization of quality reproductive health services.

Axis 3: Effective Mechanisms

Implementing efficient governance and tracking mechanisms to support the execution of the strategic plan and emergency resilience.

Sectoral Health Strategy (SSS)

Aligned with Vision 2035, the Sectoral Health Strategy aims to develop healthy and productive human capital. It is structured around five core pillars:

  • Health Promotion: Encouraging healthy behaviors and lifestyles.
  • Disease Prevention: Reducing premature mortality from preventable diseases.
  • Case Management: Reducing global mortality and lethality in health facilities.
  • Health System Strengthening: Improving infrastructure, HR, and financing.
  • Governance & Strategic Steering: Enhancing sector performance and accountability.
